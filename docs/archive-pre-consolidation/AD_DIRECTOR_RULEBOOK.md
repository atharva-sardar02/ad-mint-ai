# AD DIRECTOR RULEBOOK (Agent Training Guide)

**Purpose:** Core creative principles for producing attention-grabbing, persuasive ads through visual storytelling and emotional engagement.

**Audience:** AI agents, creative directors, storyboard artists, video production teams, and content creators working on ad campaigns.

**Last Updated:** 2025-01-17

---

## Overview

This rulebook establishes the fundamental creative principles that guide effective ad production. These rules ensure that every ad captures attention, maintains engagement, and delivers a memorable brand experience. Whether working with human directors or AI-generated content, these principles form the foundation of compelling visual advertising.

---

## Core Creative Principles

### 1. Anchor Everything on One Strong Idea

**The Creative Spine**

Identify one core visual or emotional idea that serves as the foundation for the entire ad. This "creative spine" must be:

- Clear and immediately understandable
- Visually or emotionally compelling
- Strong enough to support the entire narrative

**Application:**

- All scenes, transitions, and shots should reinforce this single idea
- Remove anything that dilutes or distracts from the core concept
- Every element should serve the central theme
- If a shot doesn't support the core idea, cut it

**Example:** For a luxury watch ad, the core idea might be "precision in motion" — every shot should reinforce this concept through visual rhythm, mechanical details, or elegant movement.

---

### 2. Lead With Emotion, Deliver With Product

**The Emotional Hook**

First evoke a specific emotion (awe, desire, joy, calm, excitement), then connect that emotion to the product reveal or payoff.

**Structure:**

1. **Emotion First:** Open with imagery that creates the desired emotional state
2. **Build Connection:** Show how the product relates to or enhances that emotion
3. **Product Payoff:** Reveal the product as the natural conclusion to the emotional journey

**Why It Works:**

- Emotion is the hook that captures attention
- Product is the conclusion that provides satisfaction
- Emotional connection creates lasting brand association

**Application:**

- Don't lead with product features
- Don't explain before you feel
- Let emotion guide the viewer to the product naturally

---

### 3. Show, Don't Tell

**Visual Storytelling Priority**

Prioritize visual storytelling over verbal explanation. Use imagery, metaphor, motion, and atmosphere to communicate value.

**Methods:**

- **Imagery:** Use powerful, evocative visuals
- **Metaphor:** Represent concepts through symbolic imagery
- **Motion:** Convey meaning through movement and rhythm
- **Atmosphere:** Create mood through lighting, color, and composition

**Avoid:**

- Over-explaining concepts with text or voiceover
- Relying on words when visuals can communicate
- Cluttering frames with unnecessary information

**Application:**

- If you can show it, don't say it
- Use visual metaphors instead of literal descriptions
- Let the viewer discover meaning through imagery

---

### 4. Use Pattern Breaks to Capture Attention

**Sustaining Viewer Engagement**

Inject small surprises every 1–3 seconds to maintain attention and increase watch time.

**Pattern Break Techniques:**

- **New angle:** Shift camera perspective unexpectedly
- **Lighting shift:** Change illumination to create visual interest
- **Movement change:** Alter the direction or speed of motion
- **Shape or color contrast:** Introduce visual contrast
- **Micro-reveal:** Show a detail or element progressively

**Why It Works:**

- Pattern breaks sustain attention
- Small surprises prevent viewer disengagement
- Regular variation increases watch time
- Creates dynamic, engaging viewing experience

**Application:**

- Plan pattern breaks at regular intervals (every 1–3 seconds)
- Vary the type of pattern break to avoid predictability
- Ensure breaks serve the core idea, not just for novelty

---

### 5. Maintain Hyper-Clear Framing

**Instant Visual Clarity**

Each shot must instantly convey what the viewer should focus on. The message must be clear even if the viewer glances for one second or watches without sound.

**Framing Principles:**

- **Clean composition:** Remove distracting elements
- **Strong silhouette:** Ensure subjects are clearly defined
- **Clear focus:** Guide the eye to the most important element
- **Legible at a glance:** Message should be immediately apparent

**Application:**

- Test each shot: Can the message be understood in one second?
- Ensure clarity without sound (silent viewing test)
- Use composition to direct attention
- Simplify when in doubt

---

### 6. Enforce Brand Visual Consistency

**Cohesive Brand Experience**

Keep a consistent color palette, mood, style, and typography throughout the ad. The final ad should feel cohesive and "from the same world."

**Consistency Elements:**

- **Color palette:** Maintain consistent color scheme
- **Mood:** Keep emotional tone consistent
- **Style:** Use consistent visual style and aesthetic
- **Typography:** Maintain consistent font choices and text treatment
- **Visual motifs:** Use repeating visual elements

**Application:**

- Define brand guidelines before production
- Reference brand style guide throughout
- Ensure transitions maintain visual continuity
- Review final cut for brand consistency

---

### 7. Assign One Purpose per Shot

**Focused Shot Function**

Every shot must have a primary job. No shot should attempt multiple unrelated purposes.

**Shot Purposes:**

- **Set mood:** Establish emotional tone
- **Build anticipation:** Create expectation or suspense
- **Show detail:** Highlight specific product features
- **Create transformation:** Show change or progression
- **Deliver the reveal:** Present the product or key moment

**Application:**

- Define the purpose of each shot before filming
- If a shot serves multiple purposes, split it into separate shots
- Ensure each shot clearly fulfills its intended function
- Remove shots without clear purpose

---

### 8. Build Momentum Toward a Payoff

**Escalating Narrative**

The story should escalate, progress, or transform. The final frame must feel earned, and the ending should be emotionally or visually satisfying.

**Momentum Techniques:**

- **Escalation:** Increase intensity or importance
- **Progression:** Show clear advancement or development
- **Transformation:** Demonstrate change or evolution
- **Anticipation:** Build toward a satisfying conclusion

**Application:**

- Plan the narrative arc before production
- Ensure each scene builds on the previous one
- Create a clear path to the payoff
- Make the ending feel inevitable yet satisfying

---

### 9. Use Purposeful Motion

**Motivated Movement**

All motion should be motivated and meaningful. Avoid chaotic movements; prioritize smoothness and intent.

**Motion Principles:**

- **Motivated:** Movement should have a clear reason
- **Meaningful:** Motion should serve the narrative or emotion
- **Smooth:** Prioritize fluid, intentional movement
- **Guided:** Use movement to direct attention

**Application:**

- Avoid random or chaotic movement
- Ensure every motion serves a purpose
- Use movement to guide the viewer's eye
- Create emotion through motion direction and speed

---

### 10. Design Memorable, Seamless Transitions

**Cinematic Cohesiveness**

Transitions should create visual continuity and enhance the narrative flow.

**Transition Techniques:**

- **Match motion:** Continue movement across cuts
- **Match color:** Use color continuity between shots
- **Match shape:** Connect shots through similar forms
- **Leverage metaphor:** Use symbolic connections (e.g., petals → bottle)

**Application:**

- Plan transitions during storyboarding
- Test transitions for visual flow
- Use transitions to reinforce the core idea
- Avoid jarring or disconnected cuts

---

### 11. Use Contrast to Add Energy

**Visual Interest Through Difference**

Add contrast in various dimensions to create visual interest and maintain attention.

**Contrast Types:**

- **Light vs. dark:** Use lighting contrast
- **Fast vs. slow:** Vary motion speed
- **Simple vs. detailed:** Alternate between minimal and complex
- **Quiet vs. bold:** Vary visual intensity
- **Soft vs. sharp:** Contrast focus and texture

**Why It Works:**

- Contrast = attention
- Variation creates visual interest
- Prevents monotony
- Guides viewer focus

**Application:**

- Plan contrast points throughout the ad
- Use contrast strategically, not randomly
- Ensure contrast serves the core idea
- Balance contrast with visual consistency

---

### 12. Add Entertainment or Delight

**Rewatchability Factor**

Include subtle moments of surprise, beauty, or cleverness that make the viewer want to rewatch.

**Delight Techniques:**

- **Surprise:** Unexpected visual moments
- **Beauty:** Aesthetically pleasing imagery
- **Cleverness:** Creative visual solutions
- **Visual metaphors:** Unexpected but meaningful connections
- **Unexpected movements:** Surprising but purposeful motion

**Application:**

- Identify opportunities for delightful moments
- Ensure delight serves the brand message
- Don't sacrifice clarity for cleverness
- Test delight moments for audience response

---

### 13. Keep the Story Simple but the Execution Unique

**Clarity Through Simplicity**

The narrative should be easy to understand. Creativity should come from visuals, motion, and atmosphere—not complexity.

**Balance:**

- **Simple message:** Easy to understand narrative
- **Distinctive execution:** Unique visual approach
- **Creative visuals:** Innovative imagery and motion
- **Clear communication:** No confusion about the message

**Application:**

- Simplify the story to its essence
- Express that simple story through creative visuals
- Avoid complex narratives that confuse
- Let creativity show in execution, not complexity

---

### 14. End With an Iconic Hero Shot

**Memorable Final Frame**

The final shot should be clean, centered, premium composition that instantly communicates the product and final emotional tone.

**Hero Shot Requirements:**

- **Clean composition:** Well-framed and balanced
- **Centered:** Product or key element clearly positioned
- **Premium:** High-quality, polished appearance
- **Well-lit:** Clear, flattering illumination
- **Highly legible:** Product and message immediately clear
- **Emotionally resonant:** Captures the desired final feeling

**Why It Matters:**

- This is the frame viewers remember
- Final impression determines brand association
- Must be immaculate and memorable

**Application:**

- Plan the hero shot during storyboarding
- Allocate sufficient time for hero shot production
- Test hero shot for immediate clarity
- Ensure hero shot reinforces the core idea

## AD DIRECTOR CHECKLIST

Use this checklist during production and review to ensure adherence to core principles.

### DO

- [ ] Use one strong core idea
- [ ] Lead with emotion, end with product
- [ ] Keep framing simple and clean
- [ ] Inject pattern breaks for attention
- [ ] Maintain cohesive style and color
- [ ] Use purposeful motion
- [ ] Create seamless or metaphorical transitions
- [ ] Build to a satisfying reveal
- [ ] End with an iconic hero shot

### DON'T

- [ ] Don't mix multiple unrelated ideas
- [ ] Don't clutter shots with too many elements
- [ ] Don't use random or chaotic movement
- [ ] Don't break the brand's visual language
- [ ] Don't over-explain with text instead of visuals
- [ ] Don't allow flat lighting or pacing
- [ ] Don't reveal too early
- [ ] Don't use scenes that don't serve the core idea

## CREATIVITY SCORE RUBRIC

Use this rubric to evaluate ad quality across ten key dimensions. Score each category from 0–10, where:

- **0–3:** Poor — Needs significant improvement
- **4–6:** Adequate — Meets basic requirements
- **7–8:** Good — Strong execution with minor gaps
- **9–10:** Excellent — Outstanding execution

### 1. Core Idea Strength (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is there one clear, strong central idea?
- Does every element support this idea?
- Is the idea compelling and memorable?
- Are there distracting elements that dilute the core idea?

**Score 9–10:** Single, powerful idea that unifies every element perfectly.

**Score 7–8:** Clear core idea with minor elements that could be better aligned.

**Score 4–6:** Core idea exists but is diluted by competing elements.

**Score 0–3:** No clear central idea or multiple conflicting ideas.

---

### 2. Emotional Impact (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Does the ad evoke a specific, strong emotion?
- Is the emotional connection clear and immediate?
- Does the emotion connect to the product naturally?
- Is the emotional payoff satisfying?

**Score 9–10:** Powerful, immediate emotional response that perfectly connects to product.

**Score 7–8:** Strong emotional impact with clear product connection.

**Score 4–6:** Some emotional content but connection to product is weak.

**Score 0–3:** Little to no emotional impact or confusing emotional tone.

---

### 3. Visual Cohesiveness (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is there consistent color palette, style, and mood?
- Do all shots feel like they're from the same world?
- Are brand visual guidelines followed?
- Is there visual continuity throughout?

**Score 9–10:** Perfectly cohesive visual language that feels unified and branded.

**Score 7–8:** Strong visual consistency with minor inconsistencies.

**Score 4–6:** Some visual consistency but noticeable breaks in style.

**Score 0–3:** Inconsistent or conflicting visual styles throughout.

---

### 4. Story Clarity (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is the narrative easy to understand?
- Can the message be grasped without sound?
- Is the story progression clear?
- Are there confusing or unclear moments?

**Score 9–10:** Crystal clear narrative that's immediately understandable.

**Score 7–8:** Clear story with minor moments that could be clearer.

**Score 4–6:** Generally understandable but some confusion.

**Score 0–3:** Confusing or unclear narrative throughout.

---

### 5. Creativity & Novelty (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is the execution unique and distinctive?
- Are there creative visual solutions?
- Does it feel fresh and original?
- Are there delightful or surprising moments?

**Score 9–10:** Highly creative and original execution that feels fresh.

**Score 7–8:** Creative approach with some unique elements.

**Score 4–6:** Some creative elements but largely conventional.

**Score 0–3:** Generic or unoriginal execution.

---

### 6. Purpose of Each Shot (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Does every shot have a clear purpose?
- Are shots focused on single objectives?
- Do shots serve the core idea?
- Are there unnecessary or purposeless shots?

**Score 9–10:** Every shot has clear, focused purpose that serves the core idea.

**Score 7–8:** Most shots have clear purpose with minor exceptions.

**Score 4–6:** Some shots lack clear purpose or serve multiple conflicting purposes.

**Score 0–3:** Many shots lack purpose or don't serve the core idea.

---

### 7. Motion & Transitions (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is all motion purposeful and meaningful?
- Are transitions seamless or metaphorically connected?
- Does movement guide attention effectively?
- Is motion smooth and intentional?

**Score 9–10:** Perfect purposeful motion with seamless, creative transitions.

**Score 7–8:** Strong motion and transitions with minor issues.

**Score 4–6:** Some purposeful motion but transitions could be improved.

**Score 0–3:** Random or chaotic movement with jarring transitions.

---

### 8. Sensory Appeal (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Does the ad engage multiple senses visually?
- Are textures, colors, and details compelling?
- Is there visual richness and depth?
- Does it create a strong sensory experience?

**Score 9–10:** Rich, multi-sensory visual experience that's highly engaging.

**Score 7–8:** Strong sensory appeal with good visual richness.

**Score 4–6:** Some sensory elements but could be more engaging.

**Score 0–3:** Flat or unengaging visual experience.

---

### 9. Attention Retention (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Are there pattern breaks to maintain attention?
- Does the pacing prevent viewer disengagement?
- Is there visual variety and contrast?
- Would viewers want to watch again?

**Score 9–10:** Highly engaging with perfect pacing and pattern breaks.

**Score 7–8:** Strong attention retention with good pacing.

**Score 4–6:** Adequate pacing but could use more pattern breaks.

**Score 0–3:** Flat pacing that fails to maintain attention.

---

### 10. Hero Shot Quality (0–10)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Is the final shot clean, centered, and premium?
- Is it well-lit and highly legible?
- Does it instantly communicate product and emotion?
- Is it memorable and iconic?

**Score 9–10:** Perfect hero shot that's iconic and memorable.

**Score 7–8:** Strong hero shot with minor improvements possible.

**Score 4–6:** Adequate hero shot but lacks impact.

**Score 0–3:** Weak or unclear hero shot that fails to deliver.

---

## Using the Rubric

**Evaluation Process:**

1. Watch the ad multiple times, focusing on different aspects
2. Score each category independently (0–10)
3. Calculate total score (out of 100)
4. Identify lowest-scoring categories for improvement
5. Use checklist to address specific issues

**Target Scores:**

- **90–100:** Excellent — Production-ready, minimal changes needed
- **80–89:** Good — Strong execution, minor refinements recommended
- **70–79:** Adequate — Meets requirements, improvements would enhance quality
- **Below 70:** Needs Work — Significant improvements required before production

## Integration with Story Types

These principles work in conjunction with the core story types:

- **Transformation Story:** Use pattern breaks to show transformation stages
- **Reveal Story:** Build momentum toward the reveal, end with hero shot
- **Journey Story:** Use purposeful motion and seamless transitions
- **Problem → Solution:** Maintain clear framing, show don't tell
- **Sensory Experience:** Focus on sensory appeal and visual cohesiveness
- **Symbolic Metaphor:** Use creative transitions and visual metaphors
- **Micro-Drama:** Lead with emotion, maintain story clarity
- **Montage:** Use pattern breaks and contrast for energy

## Notes for AI Agents

When generating ad content, ensure:

1. **Core Idea:** Identify and maintain one strong central idea throughout
2. **Emotion First:** Always lead with emotion before product reveal
3. **Visual Priority:** Minimize text, maximize visual storytelling
4. **Pattern Breaks:** Plan visual surprises every 1–3 seconds
5. **Clear Framing:** Test each shot for one-second clarity
6. **Brand Consistency:** Maintain color, style, and mood throughout
7. **Shot Purpose:** Assign clear purpose to every shot
8. **Momentum:** Build toward satisfying payoff
9. **Purposeful Motion:** Ensure all movement serves a purpose
10. **Seamless Transitions:** Use matching motion, color, or metaphor
11. **Contrast:** Add visual variety through strategic contrast
12. **Delight:** Include moments of surprise or beauty
13. **Simple Story:** Keep narrative clear, execution creative
14. **Hero Shot:** End with iconic, memorable final frame
